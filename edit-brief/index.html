01 The one thing this cut has to do

Overwhelm, then relief — and the relief has to be felt, not just seen. Front-load the chaos so densely that the viewer is a little stressed too, then rip the sound out from under them at the ocean. The silence is the payoff. If someone watches on mute, the cut still has to read as "noise → peace" through the picture and the color alone.

03 Color / vibe — Wong Kar-wai "Fallen Angels" (1995)

All three reference links are Fallen Angels (1995) aesthetic edits — that's the north star.

#fallenangels1995#wongkarwaishoegaze / dream-pop scoringstep-printed motion smear

The look: saturated neon bleeding into crushed blacks, wide-angle intimacy, and heavy smeared motion. Good news — the footage was shot at a 1/15 shutter, so the Wong Kar-wai smear is already baked in. Lean into it; don't fight it. Grade the video in two distinct temperatures so the color itself tells the story:

Chaos section — cold & toxic

Push contrast hard, crush the blacks. Cool it down with a sickly green/teal cast in the shadows (fluorescent, Muni-station energy) and let neon signage blow out into red/magenta. It should feel airless and a little nauseous.

Ocean section — warm & alive

Flip to warm the instant the sound drops. Amber / golden-hour glow, softer contrast, blacks lifted slightly so it breathes. Fallen Angels' warm-lamp interiors are the reference — but here it's the sun on water. The warmth arriving is the emotional turn; make it obvious.

Tip: a subtle film grain + tiny gate-weave/chromatic fringe on the chaos section sells the 90s WKW texture. Keep the ocean section cleaner and steadier — less grain, no shake — so the format itself calms down too.

04 Sound design

The build

  • Layer the city noise thick — voices, traffic, notifications, phone buzz — panned busy and slightly uncomfortable.
  • Rising intensity / low rumble under the last ~3 sec of chaos to signal the drop is coming.
  • The VO (below) rides over the chaos, close-mic'd and dry.

The drop & after

  • At 0:13–0:14: hard cut to near-silence. Kill everything for a beat — even a touch of room tone / ringing tone works.
  • Then fade up: ocean waves + faint meditation audio from her phone. That becomes the last sound of the film.
  • Her VO hands off to the calm — don't let city noise creep back.
On the ~1-minute audio: it's fine to cut it down — trim to the lines marked CORE below and drop the rest so it lands in ~20s. Keep natural breaths between lines; don't tighten them out. Make it make sense, don't preserve every word.

05 Editing rhythm & ending notes

Cutting

  • Chaos: cut on motion, on beat, accelerating. Whip-y, restless, jump-cuts welcome.
  • Recurring crash-out inserts as the emotional through-line — same girl, breaking down, 3–4 times.
  • Ocean: 1–2 shots only, long and static. The contrast in cutting pace matters as much as color.

Ending / motion graphics

  • Leave clean negative space (sky / water) for a text end-card.
  • Placeholder line to build around: ~~the noise~~ the world can wait. Cali.
  • Your MoGraph person can take it from the rough cut — just protect the framing headroom now.
